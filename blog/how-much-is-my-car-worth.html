Factors That Determine Your Car's Value

1. Make and Model

2. Age and Mileage

These are the two biggest factors affecting value:

  • Under 5 years / 100,000km: Highest value range
  • 5-10 years / 100-200,000km: Moderate depreciation
  • 10+ years / 200,000km+: Significant depreciation
  • 15+ years / 300,000km+: Primarily scrap/parts value

3. Condition

Honest assessment of your car's condition:

  • Excellent: Runs perfectly, no issues, well-maintained
  • Good: Minor wear, small cosmetic issues
  • Fair: Needs some repairs, visible wear
  • Poor: Major mechanical issues, significant damage
  • Non-running: Doesn't start or drive

4. Service History

A complete service history can add 10-15% to your car's value. It proves the car was properly maintained.

5. Registration Status

Registered cars are worth more than unregistered ones, as the buyer saves on safety certificate and registration costs.

How to Increase Your Car's Value

Quick Wins (Low Cost, High Impact)

  • Clean the car thoroughly inside and out
  • Gather all service records and receipts
  • Find spare keys
  • Remove personal belongings
  • Fix burnt-out lights

What NOT to Do

  • Don't spend money on major repairs
  • Don't get new tyres just to sell
  • Don't pay for professional detailing
  • Don't fix mechanical issues yourself
