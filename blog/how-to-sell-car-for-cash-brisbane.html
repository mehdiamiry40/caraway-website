2. Preparing Your Car for Sale

A little preparation can significantly increase your car's value:

Clean Your Car

  • Wash and wax the exterior
  • Vacuum and clean the interior
  • Remove personal belongings
  • Clean the windows inside and out

Gather Documentation

  • Service history and receipts
  • Registration papers
  • Owner's manual (if available)
  • Spare keys

Minor Repairs (Optional)

Fix small issues that are cheap to repair but affect perception:

  • Replace burnt-out light bulbs
  • Top up fluids
  • Fix minor scratches with touch-up paint

3. Getting the Best Price for Your Car

Several factors affect how much cash you'll receive:

Factors That Increase Value

  • Popular makes: Toyota, Mazda, Hyundai, Kia
  • Lower mileage: Under 150,000km is ideal
  • Good condition: Running well, no major damage
  • Complete service history: Shows car was maintained
  • In-demand models: SUVs and utes are popular

Factors That Decrease Value

  • High mileage (300,000km+)
  • Accident damage
  • Mechanical issues
  • Missing registration
  • Unpopular colours or models

Current Brisbane Market (2025)

Here's what you can typically expect:

  • Excellent condition: $5,000 - $15,000+
  • Good condition: $2,000 - $8,000
  • Fair condition: $500 - $3,000
  • Poor/Scrap: $100 - $1,000

4. Required Paperwork in Queensland

To legally sell your car in QLD, you'll need:

Essential Documents

  1. Photo ID: Driver's licence or passport
  2. Proof of ownership: Registration certificate or title
  3. Safety Certificate: Required for registered vehicles (seller or buyer can obtain)

What If You've Lost Your Papers?

Don't worry! Most cash for cars services can still buy your vehicle. You may need:

  • Statutory declaration of ownership
  • PPSR (Personal Property Securities Register) check
  • Additional ID verification
