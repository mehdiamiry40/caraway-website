Published: January 21, 2025 6 min read

Brisbane's subtropical climate means floods and severe storms are a fact of life. If your car has been caught in rising waters, you're facing a difficult situation. The good news? Your flood-damaged vehicle still has value, and we can help you get cash for it quickly.

Brisbane Flood Risk: A Reality Check

Brisbane has experienced significant flooding events:

  • 2011 Brisbane Floods: Thousands of vehicles destroyed
  • 2022 Flood Event: Widespread damage across SE Queensland
  • Annual Storm Season: Flash flooding affects vehicles every year

If you're reading this after a flood event, you're not alone—and there are options.

How Water Damage Affects Your Car

Depth Matters

The extent of damage depends on how high the water rose:

Water Level Typical Damage Repair Viability
Up to floor level Carpet, underbody, possible exhaust Often repairable
Seat height Electronics, seat motors, wiring Expensive to repair
Dashboard level Major systems, ECU, airbags Usually uneconomical
Full submersion Total loss – all systems Not repairable

Hidden Damage

Even "minor" flood exposure can cause serious problems:

  • Electrical corrosion: Develops over weeks/months
  • Mould and mildew: Health hazard, difficult to eliminate
  • Mechanical damage: Water in engine, transmission, differentials
  • Safety systems: Airbags, ABS, stability control may malfunction

⚠️ Important Safety Warning

Never attempt to start a flood-affected vehicle. Water in the engine can cause catastrophic damage, and electrical systems may short-circuit dangerously.

Your Options After Flood Damage

Option 1: Insurance Claim

If you have comprehensive insurance:

  • Contact your insurer immediately
  • Document damage with photos/video
  • Don't attempt repairs before assessment
  • Be prepared for "total loss" classification

Drawback: Claims process can take weeks; payout may be less than expected; premiums may increase.

Option 2: Attempted Repair

Some owners consider repair, but:

  • Costs often exceed vehicle value
  • Hidden damage may appear later
  • Resale value permanently affected
  • Safety systems may be compromised

Rule of thumb: If water reached above the floor, repair is rarely worthwhile.

Option 3: Sell for Cash

The fastest, simplest solution:

  • ✅ Instant cash payment
  • ✅ No insurance delays
  • ✅ Free removal (even if car won't start)
  • ✅ No repair costs or risks
  • ✅ Move on immediately

Why Flood Damaged Cars Still Have Value

You might wonder why anyone would buy a flood-damaged car. Here's why they retain value:

  1. Parts: Many components remain usable—panels, glass, seats, engine parts
  2. Export markets: Some overseas buyers specialise in refurbishment
  3. Scrap metal: Base metal value is unaffected by water
  4. Specialist buyers: Some vehicles are worth restoring despite damage

What to Do Right After a Flood

  1. Stay safe: Don't enter floodwaters or attempt to move the car
  2. Document everything: Photos, videos, water level marks
  3. Don't start the engine: This can cause additional damage
  4. Contact insurance: If applicable
  5. Get a cash quote: Know your options
